> More broadly, one useful way to think about the mission of a Linux
> distribution is to make all the things our users *don't* care about
> effortless and simple, so that they can invest all of their energy and

Yep.  And note that this is not to say that there is no value in
packaging things that we have chosen to run in other ways, because
different users care about the fine details of any given thing to
varying extents; we can still very usefully serve those who just want a
package that works out of the box.
